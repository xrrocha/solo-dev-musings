# Image Briefs for Naur Documentation Pessimism

**Visual Language**: Chiaroscuro / Illumination

The aesthetic should evoke light and shadow, knowledge and obscurity. Theory is represented as light/luminosity; artifacts as shadow; tacit knowledge as fog/obscurity; transmission as rays connecting sources across space/time.

**Color Palette**:
- Warm tones (gold, amber) for human elements, theory, light
- Cool tones (blue, silver) for AI elements, formal systems
- Deep shadows for artifacts, technical documentation
- Fog/mist for tacit knowledge

**Style**: Clean, modern rendering with classical composition. Think Rembrandt's use of light rendered in contemporary illustration style. Not fake-old, but timeless.

**Prompt direction**: Chiaroscuro composition, central glowing book emitting golden rays, dark background/void, multiple distant silhouetted figures receiving light, some shadowed areas suggesting incomplete transmission, dramatic lighting, scholarly atmosphere.

## 10-illumination.png

**Purpose**: Chapter header for "Conclusion"
**Dimensions**: Wide aspect ratio (~3:1 or 4:1)

**Concept**: Light passing *through* a document (not stopped by it) to illuminate a reader/figure on the other side. The document acts as medium, not barrier. Some light is lost, but enough transmits to illuminate.

**Key elements**:
- Light source on one side
- Document in the middle (translucent, not opaque)
- Light passing through document
- Figure on receiving end, illuminated
- Some light lost/scattered, but sufficient transmission
- The document is medium, not container

**Prompt direction**: Light source (sun, lamp, or abstract glow) on left, translucent document in center with light passing through, figure/reader on right illuminated by the transmitted light, document glows as light passes through it, some scattered/lost light (imperfect transmission), but the figure is sufficiently illuminated, chiaroscuro, sense of successful-if-imperfect transmission.

## Generation Notes

**Tool**: nanobanana (or similar AI image generator)

**Consistency tips**:
- Use consistent color palette across all images
- Maintain chiaroscuro aesthetic throughout
- Human elements always warm, AI elements always cool
- Theory/knowledge always luminous
- Artifacts/technical docs always in shadow
- Keep similar aspect ratios for chapter headers

**Order of generation**:
1. Start with 00-hero.png to establish the visual language
2. Generate 01 through 10 maintaining consistency
3. Generate 00-preface.png and tl-dr.png last

**Quality check**:
- Does each image embody the chapter's core concept?
- Is the light/shadow metaphor consistent?
- Would a reader understand the metaphor without reading the text?
- Do the images work at smaller sizes (mobile)?
